FRENZY

A bold website redesign for an Inc 5000, Cannes Lyon-winning B2B agency that works with Verizon, Comcast, and Slack. Their old site looked like every other agency. The new one doesn't.

Client FRENZY / Media Frenzy Global
Industry B2B Marketing, PR & Media
Scope Full Website Redesign
Timeline Oct 2025 – Mar 2026
Live Site frenzy.agency
20+ Years in Business

Inc 5000 Recognition

The Problem

An award-winning agency
with a forgettable website

FRENZY is not a typical B2B agency. They've won a Cannes Lyon. They're on the Inc 5000. Their client list includes Verizon, Comcast, Slack, Hootsuite, NCR Voyix, and Ryder. CEO Sarah Tourville built the company from London to Atlanta with zero existing U.S. connections. The team's whole identity is built around being bold, direct, and a little bit fearless.

Their website was none of those things.

The existing site looked like every other B2B agency website: safe, templated, and forgettable. It listed services without personality. It showed logos without context. It failed to answer the only question that matters when a VP of Marketing is evaluating agencies: "Why would I pick you over the fifteen other agencies in my inbox?"

close Safe, templated design that looked like every other B2B agency
close Services listed without personality or differentiation

FRENZY's Lead Client Strategist Elizabeth Williams put it plainly during kickoff:

"The current brand is just very much not who we are anymore."

The site had been built to reflect an earlier version of the company, and the business had outgrown it.

Our Approach

Bold by design, not by accident

The client set the bar high from day one.

At the design kickoff, FRENZY's President Dave McMullen gave the team a clear directive: "If I was you, I would go spicy and more spicy. I doubt you're going to show us something and go, whoa, we could never do something like that." That's rare. Most clients pull a design back toward safe. FRENZY wanted to be pushed.

We designed for bold, not safe.

The homepage opens with "Start a Frenzy" spanning the full viewport. Most B2B agency sites open with a stock photo of people in a meeting room and a tagline about "driving results." We wanted someone landing on frenzy.agency to actually stop scrolling. Dave described wanting the interplay between "big colors, big energy" and "calm black and white copy," and that dynamic runs through the entire site: high-impact visual moments followed by clean, readable content sections.

We answered the buyer's real questions.

Every section was designed to address what a B2B marketing leader is actually evaluating: What do you do? Who have you done it for? What makes you different? Can I trust you? We ordered these intentionally to match the way buyers evaluate, not the way agencies like to talk about themselves.

The brand was wide open.

Unlike many redesigns where you're working within rigid brand guidelines, FRENZY gave us full creative freedom. Elizabeth Williams confirmed there were no brand constraints worth preserving. Dave noted the FRENZY wordmark itself was iconic and trademarked, but everything else -- the colors, typography, photography treatment, visual identity -- was fair game to reimagine.

Five months of collaborative iteration.

From the October 2025 kickoff through the March 2026 launch, the project moved through homepage concept presentations, internal design reviews, timeline alignment meetings, staging, UAT, and production release. FRENZY's leadership was active throughout. The final product reflects genuine collaboration, not a design imposed on the client. The site was built on WordPress with staging and production environments, plus six months of post-launch hosting and maintenance support.

What Made This Different

We wrote their launch captions.
They didn't change a word.

Two things stood out. First, the client's posture. Most clients hedge. Dave McMullen told us at kickoff to go "spicy and more spicy." That trust gave us the permission to push the full-viewport "Start a Frenzy" hero, a move that could have easily been killed in review but ended up defining the entire site's identity. FRENZY's team pushed the work forward instead of pulling it back, and the result is a site that stands out in a category where almost nothing does.

Second, the launch kit. We wrote three social media caption options for their launch announcement, each from a different angle, all in FRENZY's voice. For a PR and marketing agency, writing their captions is a high-wire act. If the voice is off, they'll notice immediately. Sarah's team accepted the captions without edits. That told us we'd internalized the brand well enough to speak as them, not for them.
