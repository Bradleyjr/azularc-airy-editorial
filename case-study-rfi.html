RF Installations

A complete website redesign for a $70M national installation company with 27+ years of experience. The site wasn't telling their story. Now it does.

Client RF Installations LLC
Industry Home Improvement Services
Scope Full Website Redesign
Timeline Launched March 2026
Live Site rfinstallations.com
27+ Years in Business
1,500+ Contractors
400K+ Annual Installations

The Problem

A $70M company with a website
that undersold them

RF Installations coordinates over 400,000 home improvement installations a year across 40 states. They work with major retailers, manage a network of 1,500+ independent contractors, and recently earned #20 on Qualified Remodeler's Top 500 list. By every operational measure, RFI is a serious national company.

Their website told a different story.

The existing site was built on a dated design that hadn't kept pace with the company's growth. Service pages read like internal documentation rather than client-facing content. There was no clear path for the three distinct audiences who visit the site: retail partners evaluating RFI as an installation provider, independent contractors looking to join the network, and end consumers checking on their upcoming installation.

close Dated design that didn't reflect $70M+ scale

During the initial strategy workshop, RFI's own team called out a telling problem: they had years of credibility data, compliance credentials, and impressive scale numbers -- but none of it was visible on the website.

They had proof and weren't using it.

Our Approach

Two concepts, audience-first IA,
and proof up front

We started with two distinct homepage concepts.

The first leaned into a grid and blueprint-style layout with animated scrolling sections, noise textures, and schematic illustrations tying into the construction world. The second took a more structured, blocky approach with tabbed content, neutral backgrounds, and a slab serif typeface that communicated stability without being boring. Both were designed as conversation tools, not final designs, to gauge where the client's comfort level landed and which direction resonated.

We restructured the IA around audiences, not departments.

Retail partners need to see scale, compliance credentials, and geographic reach. Contractors need to understand the benefits of joining the network and how to apply. Consumers need reassurance that their installation will go smoothly. Each audience now has a clear entry point and a tailored content path. The services section stays on a single page rather than splitting into separate solution pages, since all services funnel to the same offering.

We led with proof, not promises.

The homepage was rebuilt to lead with the numbers that matter: 27+ years, 1,500+ contractors, 400,000+ annual installations, 40+ states. These aren't buried in body copy -- they're the first thing a visitor sees. This decision came directly from the strategy workshop where the client acknowledged they had the credibility but weren't showcasing it. We also introduced a "Transforming Spaces, Delivering Results" project carousel to visually demonstrate installation quality across product categories.

We explored a brand identity refresh.

In addition to the website, we experimented with a modernized logo concept and opened a conversation about whether the company should position itself as "RFI" or "RF Installations" going forward. This wasn't in the original scope, but it was the kind of added value that strengthened the relationship and helped the client think bigger about their brand.

What Made This Different

The launch kit, plus a brand conversation they didn't expect

Most agencies hand over a URL and move on. We delivered a complete launch package: branded mockup images styled for social, three caption options written in RFI's brand voice, and a feedback form. This turned the launch from a quiet go-live into a marketing moment the client could actually use.

We also went beyond the original scope by exploring a modernized logo concept and opening a conversation about brand positioning (RFI vs. RF Installations). None of that was in the contract. But for a company going through a major web presence upgrade, it was the right moment to ask the bigger question about who they want to be. That kind of added value is what turns a project into a relationship.
