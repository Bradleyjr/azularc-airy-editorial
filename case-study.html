Government & Courts

CaseHub

Replacing paper-based court filing across 12 counties with a unified digital platform.

Client Georgia Courts
Industry Government
Timeline 14 months
Services Case Management, UX, Data Migration
The Challenge

What we walked into

Georgia's court system was running on paper. Not metaphorically -- literally. Fax machines handled case transfers between counties. Filing cabinets held the only copies of critical legal documents. The software they did use was built in 2004 and hadn't been meaningfully updated since.

Twelve counties, each with their own filing procedures, their own workarounds, their own version of "how things get done." Compliance requirements were changing faster than any manual process could keep up with. Citizens waited weeks for updates on their cases -- because no one could find the paperwork.

The clerks weren't the problem. They were doing heroic work inside a system that was designed for a different century. They needed tools that matched their dedication.

close Paper-based filing across all 12 counties
close Legacy software from 2004, no longer supported
close Fax-dependent inter-county case transfers
close Weeks-long wait times for citizen case updates

Court clerks managed thousands of filings per week using processes unchanged since the early 2000s.

The Approach

How we got there

01
Weeks 1-4

Discovery & Audit

We spent four weeks embedded in three counties, sitting with clerks, watching their workflows, and mapping every process -- from initial filing to case closure. We documented 47 distinct workflows and identified 23 that could be automated immediately.

02
Weeks 5-8

System Architecture

We designed a multi-tenant platform that could accommodate each county's specific needs while enforcing state-level compliance standards. The architecture had to handle massive document volumes, concurrent users across counties, and air-tight security for sensitive legal data.

03
Weeks 9-44

Iterative Build

We built in 2-week sprints, showing working software to county clerks every cycle. Their feedback shaped everything -- from the dashboard layout to the document upload flow. By month six, two pilot counties were running on the new system alongside the old one.

04
Weeks 45-60

Phased Rollout

We rolled out county-by-county, migrating data, training staff, and running parallel systems until each county was confident. No big-bang. No downtime. No one lost a single filing in the transition. The last county went live three weeks ahead of schedule.
